> This article aims to explain the definition and classification of AGI

I've wanted to talk about `AGI` for a long time, and now I finally have the time to discuss `AGI`.

## What is AGI
Without further ado, let me show you a picture:
![AGI](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/byolio/mytc@main/img/NGS.png)
By definition, `AGI` refers to `AI` that can achieve human-level performance on any task. This specifically includes:
`language understanding and generation`, `learning and reasoning`, `perception and understanding`, `emotion and social skills`, `creativity`, etc.

## Classification of AGI
Regarding the current `AGI`, `OpenAI` and `Google`'s `DeepMind` have provided different answers:

### OpenAI:
Let's first talk about `OpenAI`'s level classification:
![OpenAI AGI](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/byolio/mytc@main/img/openAILevel.png)
As shown in the figure, OpenAI divides `AGI` into five levels:
* Level 1: `AI` capable of natural language conversation, able to conduct basic dialogues
* Level 2: `AI` with human-like reasoning abilities, capable of solving problems across a wide range of domains
* Level 3: `AI` systems that can take actions based on human instructions or independently (using `agents`)
* Level 4: `AI` systems that can help invent new ideas and contribute to human knowledge
* Level 5: `AI` systems that can independently complete all work of an entire organization

It can be seen that currently `OpenAI`'s classification of `AGI` levels is quite vague, providing almost no precise criteria for judgment. Therefore, we will mainly look at `DeepMind`'s side.

### DeepMind:
![DeepMind AGI](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/byolio/mytc@main/img/deepmindGoogleLevel.png)
It is clear that compared to `OpenAI`'s level classification, `DeepMind`'s classification is much clearer:
* Level 0: No AI: At this stage, there is no `AI`, only simple programs like calculators or compilers.
* Level 1: Emerging: `AI` at this stage is better than non-experts. Currently, all `AI` falls into this level.
* Level 2: Competent: In certain narrow domains, `AI` performs better than `50%` of practitioners.
* Level 3: Expert: `AI` performs better than `90%` of practitioners.
* Level 4: Virtuoso: `AI` demonstrates extremely high skills, surpassing `99%` of practitioners.
* Level 5: Superhuman: `ASI` (Artificial Super Intelligence), where `AI` capabilities exceed `100%` of all humans.


## o1-preview Level
As the model o1-preview (previously codenamed `Strawberry`) released by `OpenAI` on `September 12, 2024`,
`OpenAI` claims it uses a new `scaling law`.
![o1-preview](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/byolio/mytc@main/img/o1-preview-theory.png)
From the image, it can be seen that the new `scaling law` generated by `OpenAI` refers to the use of `Chain of Thought` combined with `agent` methods. However, since its use of `agents` is still in its infancy, it currently lies between `level 3` and `level 2` in `OpenAI`'s classification, but still belongs to `level 2`. In `DeepMind`'s classification, tests show it still cannot surpass `50%` of practitioners, so it remains at `DeepMind`'s `level 1`.


## Conclusion
`AGI` is a complex concept involving multiple layers of technology and capabilities. Currently, we are still in the exploratory phase of `AGI` and cannot fully achieve the goal of `AGI`. Therefore, we don't need to worry too much about the impact of `AGI` on us.
The above are my simple views on the definition and classification of `AGI`. Although I haven't delved deeply, I hope it is helpful to you.
