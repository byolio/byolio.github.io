> This article aims to explain the definition and classification of AGI

I have wanted to talk about `AGI` for a long time, and now I finally have the time to discuss `AGI`.

## What is AGI
Without further ado, let me show you a picture:
![AGI](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/byolio/mytc@main/img/NGS.png)
By definition, `AGI` refers to `AI` that can reach human-level performance on any task. This includes: 
`language understanding and generation`, `learning and reasoning`, `perception and understanding`, `emotion and social ability`, `creativity`, etc.

## Classification of AGI
Regarding the current `AGI`, `OpenAI` and `Google`'s `DeepMind` have given different answers:

### OpenAI:
Let's first talk about `OpenAI`'s level classification:
![openAI AGI](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/byolio/mytc@main/img/openAILevel.png)
As can be seen from the figure, OpenAI has divided `AGI` into five levels:
* Level 1: `AI` with natural language conversation ability, capable of basic conversation
* Level 2: `AI` with human-like reasoning ability, capable of solving problems in a wide range of fields
* Level 3: `AI` system that can take actions based on human instructions or independently (using `agents`)
* Level 4: `AI` system that can help invent new ideas and contribute to human knowledge
* Level 5: `AI` system that can independently complete all work of an entire organization

It can be seen that currently `OpenAI`'s classification of `AGI` levels is very vague, providing almost no accurate judgment, so we mainly look at `DeepMind`'s side.

### DeepMind:
![deepmind AGI](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/byolio/mytc@main/img/deepmindGoogleLevel.png)
It can be clearly seen that compared to `OpenAI`'s level classification, `DeepMind`'s level classification is clearer:
* Level 0: No AI: At this point, there is no `AI`, only simple programs, such as calculators or compilers.
* Level 1: Emerging: `AI` at this stage is stronger than non-practitioners; currently, all `AI` belongs to this level.
* Level 2: Competent: In some narrow fields, `AI` performs better than `50%` of practitioners.
* Level 3: Expert: `AI` performs better than `90%` of practitioners.
* Level 4: Virtuoso: `AI` demonstrates extremely high skills, surpassing `99%` of practitioners.
* Level 5: Superhuman: `ASI` (Artificial Super Intelligence), at this point, AI capability surpasses `100%` of all humans.


## o1-preview Level
As a model released by `OpenAI` on `2024.9.12`, o1-preview (previously codenamed `Strawberry`), 
`OpenAI` publicly claimed that it uses a new `scaling law`
![o1-preview](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/byolio/mytc@main/img/o1-preview-theory.png)
From the picture, it can be seen that the new `scaling law` generated by `OpenAI` refers to the method of using `Chain of Thought` combined with `agent`. However, since its use of `agent` is only in its infancy, it is currently between `level 3` and `level 2` in `OpenAI`'s classification, but still belongs to the `level 2` stage. In `DeepMind`'s classification, through testing, it still cannot surpass `50%` of practitioners, so it remains at `level 1` in `DeepMind`.


## Conclusion
`AGI` is a complex concept involving multiple levels of technology and capabilities. Currently, we are still in the exploration stage of `AGI` and cannot fully achieve the goal of `AGI`. Therefore, we don't need to worry too much about the impact of `AGI` on us.\
The above are my simple views on the definition and classification of `AGI`. Although not deeply discussed, I hope it is helpful to you.
